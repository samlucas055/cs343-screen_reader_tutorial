Screen Reader Tutorial for Windows Narrator

This tutorial is for Window's version 10 Narrator using Firefox web browser version 109.0.1.

Check out Screen Reader Controls for more information about Narrator and navigation keys.

The screen reader can be enabled by pressing the windows key, control key, and enter key.

The screen reader can be stopped by pressing the windows, control, and enter keys. Additionally, to exit the screen reader press the escape key.

Controls for Common Tasks

It is important to note that the Caps lock key and insert key serve as the Narrator key by default. These keys can be changed by going into Narrator settings. To open Narrator settings press the Windows Key + Ctrl + N

  • To navigate through a page, press the tab key.
  • To get Narrator to read a certain item press Narrator and tab.
  • To read and entire page, press Ctrl, Narrator, and I keys.
  • To repeat a phrase, press the Narrator and X keys.
  • To increase voice speed, press the Narrator and + keys, and to decrease voice speed, press the Narrator and - keys.
  • To action an item, press the Narrator and enter keys.

How to Navigate Screen Reader Friendly Website

WebAIM

WebAIM is a screen reader friendly website. Activate Narrator, then press the tab key and then the enter key to skip to main content. Then, press the tab key to navigate through the links in the table of contents. Next, use the down arrow key to have Narrator read the next group of text to you. Narrator will read text until it encounters a link or the end of the paragraph, whichever happens first. To read text further up the page, press the up arrow key until you reach the text you want Narrator to read.

How to Navigate a Screen Reader Not-So-Friendly Website

Some websites are not so friendly when using a Screen Reader. For example, Black Sheep Coffee, a local coffe shop in Harrisonburg has a website for their customers to view. However, when you go to access it using a Screen Reader, it isn't the most efficient. Here is a step by step tutorial of trying to navigate Black Sheep Coffee's website.

  1. Turn on Navigator.
  2. Go to Black Sheep Coffee
  3. Navigate through the page using tab or your hotkey of choice.
  4. As you will notice when you first tab through the page it will take you to two links that are positioned at the top of the page. They are links to download applications, but unfortunately, it doesn't describe it as so.
  5. Next, you will be directed to the header of the page which outlines the website for you as links.
  6. As you continue to tab through the content of the page, it will take you to several more links, many of which do not have a description.
  7. The website has a lot of lovely photos, however, most of them don't have figure descriptions to be read to the user.
  8. As you will eventually find out by navigating through the page, you will never get to the main content. This is poor design for a Screen Reader because customers will never be able to reach important information about the coffee shop.
